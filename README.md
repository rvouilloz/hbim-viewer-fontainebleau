# HBIM Viewer Fontainebleau

## Explore the Demo

https://rvouilloz.github.io/hbim-viewer-fontainebleau/

## About

HBIM Viewer Fontainebleau is a research project conducted as a collaboration between CIMS (Carleton University), MHA (Univ. Grenoble-Alpes, ENSAG) and CNPA (EPFL).

The experimentation aims at hybridizing the Digital Survey and the BIM of a same asset into a web visualization, with the Fontainebleau horseshoe staircase as a case study.

It uses the library 3DTilesRendererJS to load the survey as 3D Tiles, and That Open Engine (ex IFC.js) to load the BIM model as an IFC and to interact with metadata, in a shared Three.js environment.
The IFC was created from the survey mesh in Blender / Bonsai (ex BlenderBIM); the research explores the concept of Native IFC, using the open standard for BIM as a native editing format from modelisation to dissemination.

## Paper

The research is explained in an academic paper to be presented at ISPRS Congress 2026: Bridging Geometric Gaps between Digital Survey and BIM through Open-Source 3D Tiles - IFC Integration (Raphaël Vouilloz, Ken Percy, Nicolas Arellano Risopatron, Sabrina Liu, Philippe Marin, Stephen Fai)

The research was funded in part by the Social Sciences and Humanities Research Council (SSHRC) of Canada, Partnership Talent Program (895-2015-1018), as a participation to the "New Paradigms, New Tools" program of the CIMS Laboratory in Ottawa.
